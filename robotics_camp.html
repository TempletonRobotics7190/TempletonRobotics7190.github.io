About The Camp

This camp will be run at Templeton Secondary School. The primary objective of this camp is to teach fundamental engineering concepts through practical, hands-on experience, while also fostering a sense of teamwork among attendees. It is designed to teach engineering concepts through hands on work, and enchourage teamwork. In building a robot, attendees will learn: engineering skills, wiring, technical skills, and design.

  • Location: Templeton Secondary School
  • Grades: 8-12
  • Expirience Required: None
  • Hours: 9:00 am - 12:00 pm
  • Week: August 14-18
  • Cost: $300

Throughout the duration of the camp, participants will be engaged in an immersive experience of designing and building robots. They will learn valuable engineering skills that will help them to better understand the mechanical and electronic components that go into constructing these innovative machines. Additionally, they will have the opportunity to learn the intricacies of wiring, which is a crucial component of the construction process.

Skills

Engineering

Building a robot can provide attendees with invaluable experience in the field of engineering. Attendees will gain hands-on experience in building and troubleshooting a robot, while also developing problem-solving skills and critical thinking abilities.

Wiring

Our program helps attendees understand the key components of robots, including mechanical and electronic elements, and how they work together to create a functioning robot.

Design Skills

Overall, by experimenting with different coding and designing techniques, a robotics camp provides a collaborative and engaging environment for attendees to develop their design skills and bring their creative ideas to fruition.
